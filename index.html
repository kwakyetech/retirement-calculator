Calculation Methods & Tips

4% Withdrawal Rule

A widely-used rule suggesting retirees can safely withdraw 4% of their retirement savings annually without depleting their nest egg over a 30-year retirement.

Annual Income = Total Savings × 0.04

Income Replacement Method

Calculates needed savings based on your desired percentage of pre-retirement income. Most financial experts recommend replacing 75-85% of pre-retirement income.

Required Savings = (Desired Income) / Withdrawal Rate

Compound Interest Projection

Projects the future value of your current savings plus regular contributions, accounting for compound growth over time.

FV = PMT × [((1 + r)^n - 1) / r] + PV × (1 + r)^n

Retirement Planning Tips

  • Start saving early to benefit from compound interest
  • Increase contributions when you get raises or bonuses
  • Consider maximizing employer 401(k) matching
  • Diversify investments appropriate for your age and risk tolerance
  • Plan for healthcare costs in retirement
  • Consider working part-time in early retirement years
